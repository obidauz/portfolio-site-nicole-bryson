overview:

InkMe was a project for my CareerFoundry UX Immersion course, meant to teach me the basics of UX Design thinking while building a prototype I could be passionate about. The project took approximately 6 months to complete and I was the solo UX Designer.

InkMe is a mobile app created to help users find tattoo inspiration, chat with potential artists, find local shops, and participate in the tattoo community. The main goal of inkMe is to reduce potential regret after getting a tattoo, which will hopefully be achieved through use of the features provided.

tasks:

  • User Interviews
  • User Personas
  • User Flows
  • Wireframing and Prototyping
  • Usability Testing

tools:

  • Balsamiq
  • Lucidchart
  • Sketch
  • ProtoPie

problem statement

Our tattoo app users need a way to find local, affordable tattoo artists that specialize in their desired style because they want to work with an artist they enjoy and who will provide them with a service they want to help minimize the possibility of regret. We will know this to be true when we see users who continue to use our app to find inspiration for their pieces, who refer our service to others, and who are happy with their tattoo after using our app.

USER PERSONAS

I conducted user surveys and interviews with 3 potential users. I then created two user personas based on the information gathered.

meet rafael

  • Age: 38
  • Career: Coffee shop owner
  • Status: Married
  • Children: 8 year old son
  • Location: Brooklyn, NY

needs and goals

  • Wants to get a sleeve that will cover up previous tattoos.
  • Needs to find an artist that is good at cover up work.
  • Wants a sleeve in a Neo Traditional style and needs an artist that specializes in that style.
  • Wants to be able to speak to an artist about possible design ideas to make sure they are on the same page.

frustrations

  • Frustrated by AR tattoo apps that show the tattoo on your skin as they do not work on top of all of his tattoos.
  • Does not like when apps are not accessible across multiple devices.

motivations

  • Would like to get a sleeve that represents the business that he worked hard to build.
  • Appreciates his tattoos that he got when he was younger as learning experiences, but realizes now that he wants tattoos that really mean something to him.
  • Enjoys the process of getting tattoos and is motivated to create his sleeve with a talented artist.

daily activities

  • Spends his mornings getting his son ready for school before opening his shop.
  • Works at and runs his shop almost every day.
  • Spends a lot of time on his computer at work.
  • Has very little time for social media beyond advertising for his business.

meet chrissy

  • Age: 23
  • Career: Dog walking while attending grad school
  • Status: Single with 2 dogs and a cat
  • Location: San Diego, CA

needs and goals

  • Wants to get a second tattoo.
  • Needs to find an artist that can help her find the perfect tattoo design.
  • Wants a hyper-realistic tattoo and needs an artist who is good but affordable.

frustrations

  • She was very disappointed when getting her first tattoo, a paw print on her hip, as the artist did not advise her in doing anything more creative.
  • Finds it very difficult to get any information about tattoos and specific skin tones, such as what type and color of tattoo would work best for her.

motivations

  • Got her first tattoo on a whim on her 19th birthday but has been thinking about this next tattoo for 2 years.
  • Wants her next tattoo to be a portrait of her first dog, Arya, and is very drawn to hyper-realistic styles.
  • Is concerned about how her parents would react to the tattoo so wants it to fit in a place that she can keep hidden if she wants.

daily activities

  • Balances her daily schedule between dog walking/pet sitting and studying for the GRE.
  • On social media frequently promoting her dog walking business as well as keeping up with her college friends.
  • Has a laptop at home but only uses it to watch tv.
  • Constantly on the go and uses her phone for everything from GPS, to schedule keeping, to playing music.

usability testing

Using the high-fidelity wireframes, I conducted usability testing with 6 peers. These peers were between the ages of 25-35, had tattoos or were looking to get a tattoo, and had varying degrees of technical savvy.

RETROSPECTIVE

what went well

InkMe was my first full UX Designer project for my UX Immersion course. I was very happy with my final result as I had never designed an app before and was using all of the tools for the first time. I really enjoyed iterating upon my designs, taking my user feedback, and improving the functionality of the app. I was especially happy with my realization to move from the clunky pop-out menu to the navigation bar of a native app.

what didn't go well

The terminology of the menu items and the organization was very hard to figure out and work with my users. Learning to let my original attachments go was a struggle. I also felt somewhat limited in what I could create with Sketch and how I could bring my prototypes to life, though this had more to do with my own lack of experience and not a problem with the program itself. I feel I still had a lot to learn in terms of image placement, color choices, and typography.

future improvements

I would love to add more pages to the app, creating what it would be like to actually search or browse through the pages of styles. I would also try to simplify and clean up the look of the app overall. I had multiple comments from peers about how they expected a darker theme. I would potentially create a dark theme version of the app to make it edgier and more fitting for tattoo searching.

dark theme redesign

Following my UI specialty course, I decided to go back and tweak my original design to create an edgy, dark look. I took a few key pages and redesigned them in Sketch. I also took this as an opportunity to create a realistically interactive prototype and learn how to use ProtoPie in the process. I am very pleased with the new look of the app, as are my previous users who originally tested the app. One actually said, “It looks like a real app now!”. I plan to recreate each page and build a fully interactive prototype. Stay tuned!
